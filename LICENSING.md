# Licensing

MARROW is **dual-licensed**: open source under the AGPL, with a commercial license available for uses that the AGPL doesn't fit.

## Open source — AGPL-3.0

The source in this repository is licensed under the **GNU Affero General Public License, version 3** (see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)).

In plain terms, you are free to:

- **use** MARROW for any purpose,
- **run and self-host** it, including in your own organization,
- **study, modify, and redistribute** it.

The core obligation of the AGPL: if you **modify** MARROW and **make it available to others over a network** (for example, running a modified version as a hosted service), you must **make your modified source available** to those users under the AGPL as well. This is the "network copyleft," and it is deliberate — it keeps improvements open and prevents a closed, hosted fork from competing against the community without giving back.

If self-hosting under these terms works for you, you need nothing else. This is the right path for most users, internal deployments, and the sovereignty-first audience MARROW is built for.

## Commercial license

A separate **commercial license** is available for cases where the AGPL's obligations are a poor fit, for example:

- embedding MARROW in a **proprietary or closed-source product**,
- offering a **hosted/SaaS service** built on MARROW **without** releasing your modifications under the AGPL,
- any situation requiring terms (warranty, indemnity, support) that an open-source license does not provide.

The commercial license grants the same software under terms suited to closed/commercial distribution, decoupled from the AGPL's source-sharing requirement.

**To obtain a commercial license:** contact **licensing@<your-domain>** (replace with the real address).

## Contributions

Contributions are accepted under the project's inbound license and a contributor agreement that lets the project offer the dual-licensed (open + commercial) model — see [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md). This is what makes the commercial option possible while keeping the open-source project genuinely open.
